Spatio-Temporal Traffic Forecasting with Graph Neural Networks

Forecasting hourly traffic speeds across Berlin's loop-detector network with an attention-based temporal graph convolutional network (A3T-GCN), built in PyTorch on the city's open VMZ dataset. Ongoing.  ·  GitHub repo →

The problem

Traffic speed at one detector depends on what happened upstream an hour ago — the signal is inherently both spatial and temporal. The project builds an A3T-GCN model to forecast hourly speeds 3, 6, and 12 hours ahead across 454 detectors, benchmarked honestly against last-value, historical-average, and per-node LSTM baselines, so any GNN gain is measured against the strongest simple alternatives rather than a strawman.

Data pipeline: from 5,000+ raw CSVs to a clean tensor

  • Wrote the pipeline turning 5,000+ monthly CSVs into a (T, N, F) PyTorch tensor with a parallel validity mask.
  • Steps: quality filtering, short-gap forward-fill, seasonal-mean imputation, and cyclic time encoding — with the mask preserving which values are observed vs. imputed so the model never trains on fiction as if it were fact.

The bug worth telling: a silent extraction error had been reducing a full year of usable data down to a single month — no exception, no warning, just a quietly tiny dataset. Catching it required distrusting a pipeline that appeared to work. It's the kind of failure that inflates validation metrics while gutting the model, and the reason I now treat row counts as assertions, not log lines.

Building the spatial graph

  • Projected detectors into Berlin UTM with GeoPandas, computed pairwise distances with SciPy, and weighted edges with a Gaussian kernel thresholded at 2 km.
  • A modeling decision backed by data: chose detector-level over pole-level nodes after a correlation study showed sibling detectors on the same pole carry independent direction and lane signal — aggregating them would have thrown away real information.
